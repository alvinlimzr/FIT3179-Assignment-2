Health & Wealth: a short data story

This page walks you through three views of income and longevity. Start with a world snapshot, then explore how the two relate, and finally compare a few countries side by side.

1) Global snapshot — World GDP per capita (2024)

What to look for: a high-income belt across North America, Western Europe and Oceania, and gaps across parts of Africa and South Asia. This sets the scene before we connect income with health.

2) Relationship — Life expectancy vs GDP per capita

The curve flattens. Early income gains often come with big improvements in life expectancy; at higher incomes, the extra years arrive more slowly. Try scrubbing the years and spot countries that outperform their income peers.

3) Focus countries — life expectancy (selected year)

Takeaways

  • Income is strongly related to longevity, with diminishing returns at higher GDP per capita.
  • Policy and health systems matter: countries at similar incomes can have very different outcomes.
  • Development paths vary—some countries convert growth into health faster than others.
